Vegan Yellow Thai Curry

A take-away classic that's so quick and easy to hop from wok to plate at home, you're guaranteed to save yourself a trip to the delivery menu. This is our vegan broccoli stir-fry with "beef" seitan.

The broccoli is divided into individual florets and cut into bite-sized pieces, because even as big broccoli admirers, we don't exactly want to chew on a whole tree. The vegetables now come with something in the pan, is steamed briefly with the lid closed and then quenched under running cold water, so the intense green color is retained.

The seitan and onion are now sautéed together, stirring frequently, until the seitan becomes slightly crispy here and there and the onion begins to caramelize.
At this point, the well-drained broccoli comes back into play and may now also enjoy some direct heat and brown a bit. I have never understood the principle of distinguished paleness. Especially not when cooking.

Ingredients

  • 7 g ginger grated
  • 1 clove garlic grated
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 300 g seitan beef
  • 1 onion
  • 300 g broccoli
  • 2 tbsp peanut oil

For sauce

  • 4 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin optional
  • 1 tbsp. sesame oil
  • 1 tsp. raw cane sugar
  • 1 clove garlic grated

Steps

  1. For the marinade, mix grated ginger and garlic with soy sauce and cornstarch. Cut seitan into thin slices, add to marinade and mix well.
  2. Slice onion into strips, divide broccoli into florets and cut into bite-sized pieces if desired. For the sauce, mix all ingredients with 75 ml of water and set aside.
  3. Add about 100 ml of water to a hot wok or skillet, steam broccoli for 1 ½-2 minutes with lid on high heat until most of the water has evaporated. Drain, rinse under cold water and drain well.
  4. Return wok to stove, add peanut oil and stir-fry seitan and onion over high heat for 3 minutes until both are lightly caramelized.
  5. Add broccoli and stir-fry for another 3 minutes. Then add sauce, bring to a boil while stirring, and simmer for about 2 minutes until sauce is slightly thickened.
  6. Top with toasted sesame seeds and serve with jasmine rice, rice noodles or wheat noodles.
