Vegan Yellow Thai Curry

Extra-creamy, milder compared to the red and green Thai curry and in a maximum of half an hour it is already on the table. This is our vegan yellow Thai curry with vegan "chicken". Could be your new go-to curry, I have a feeling.

While probably every curry fan can imagine that the red Thai curry or the spice paste has its color from the red chili peppers and it is only obvious that it is the green chilies in the green variant that the vegetables finally swim in a discreet green, fresh-looking sauce, curry fans in particular, who are somewhat more sensitive to spiciness, now have a lot of respect for a possibly even spicier sauce after eating red ("Yes, I thought it would be spicy based on the color") and green ("Holy shit, that's even spicier! ") now respect a possibly even more lasting impression of yellow. Burns twice and all that.

If you belong to the described group, I can reassure you. Our yellow Thai curry is milder with lots of coconut milk and warm spices, gets its intense yellow color from turmeric and thus also means well with "you". But even chili freaks, as we are, should not close the browser window right now. On the one hand, there is still at least a spicy amount of hot peppers in the pot, and on the other hand, you can still have your pear smoked individually at the table - freshly chopped chilies, our chili vinegar or of course Sriracha may never be missing at the spicy-inclusive table. Besides, it just tastes incredibly good even with less steam and between us - and let's put the chili giver aside, if we're honest, it has a bit of tuning Golf with underbody lighting - that's what matters in the end.

Ingredients

For the curry paste

  • 4 shallots
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1 stalk of lemongrass
  • 1 red Thai chili
  • 1 tsp galangal powder or 10 g fresh galangal
  • 15 g fresh turmeric root
  • 15 g coriander
  • 2 tbsp curry powder
  • 4 tbsp. soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp table vinegar
  • 2 tsp. light miso paste

For the curry

  • 2 onions
  • 225 g carrot
  • 300 g potato
  • 2 tbsp peanut oil
  • 1-2 pkg LikeMeat Like Chicken
  • 3 tbsp curry paste
  • 400 ml coconut milk
  • 15 g coriander

Steps

For the curry paste

  1. Remove dry outer leaves from the lemongrass, cut off the stalk and the tip, and roughly cut the rest into rings. Peel the turmeric and coarsely chop together with the shallots, garlic, chili and coriander.
  2. Puree all ingredients, except miso paste, in a large mortar or food processor to a fine paste. Then stir in miso paste.
  3. The curry paste can be kept well sealed in the refrigerator for up to 14 days.

For the curry

  1. Cut onions, carrot and potatoes into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Add oil to a hot pot, saut√© Like Chicken over medium-high heat for 2 minutes, stirring frequently, then remove from pot and set aside.
  3. Add curry paste and 3 tablespoons coconut milk to pot and stir-fry over medium-high heat for 2 minutes. Add vegetables, deglaze with remaining coconut milk and 200 ml water, bring to a boil and simmer over low-medium heat with lid on for 15 minutes. Return Like Chicken to pot and let simmer for another 5 minutes.
  4. Top with cilantro greens and serve with jasmine rice.
